Title It needs a compelling (and ideally, short) title. Conference attendees will likely glance at the timetable and not necessarily read all the abstracts - have keywords in the title that will grab the attention. Maybe indicate the level required. Good examples are things like:

  • Scala for Java Developers
  • Intro to [Technology X]
  • [Technology X]: Beyond the Basics
  • How [Company Y] scaled to [some insane metric]
  • (Martijn’s current talk)
  • 6 million transactions a second
  • AngularJS, Java, Groovy, MongoDB working together

Abstract Needs to explain: - Who the talk is aimed at - Vaguely what it’s going to cover

Each conference has a different set of fields to fill in for the talk submissions. For some, it all has to be in the abstract, for others, they’ll ask for a behind-the-scenes look at the talk, or a structure of how you’re going to present it. For your talk, whether you’ve written it yet or not, you’ll need to know:

  • Who is it aimed at?
  • What will they learn?
  • Which buzz words will grab attention?
  • What story will you tell?

Past performance - Get videoed. Even if you’re not fantastic, you’re miles ahead of an unknown already - Get a named event on your CV. Pair if necessary. - have a blog if you don’t have videos - LinkedIn - Twitter - Be linked to A Name: a company, a person, a conference, a user group.
